Park and Ride Proposed Sites

Note The above map has been recreated from the original images provided by the council. There may be some minor deviation from the official plans. The information below is taken from the consultation meeting documentation.

Name Description Benefits Constraints
Site A - Land east of A4/A46 junction This site is closest to the city with access from London Road West, with the majority of the site being east of the London Road roundabout.
  • Near to the city
  • Visual impact could be reduced by landscaping and planting
  • Could provide access to the river for Park and Sail
  • High risk of flooding and would require placement flood capacity if developed
  • Part of the River Avon Site of Nature Conservation Interest
  • Inside City of Bath World Heritage Site and Bath Conservation Area
  • Would create a need to replace the existing playing fields elsewhere
  • Split site with part to the west of the A4
  • Could not be served by a new rail station
  • In private ownership
Site B - Land west of Mill Lane The site is located adjacent to the A4 Bathampton Bypass to the west of Mill Lane with access via a new junction onto the A4 dual carriageway. The design of this new junction will be reviewed to see if it can be located west of Mill Lane Bridge possibly using a slip road.
  • Not considered at risk of flooding
  • Outside City of Bath World Heritage Site
  • Visual impact could be reduced by landscaping and planting
  • Could be served by a new rail station
  • Could provide access to the river for Park and Sail
  • Requires a new access from the A4
  • Might request access from adjoining site with a tunnel under Mill Lane
  • In private ownership
Site F - Land east of Mill Lane This site is located adjacent to the A4 Bathampton Bypass to the east of Mill Lane. Planning permission for this site was granted in May 2009 with access via a new junction on the A4 dual carriageway but was not progressed. The site is owned by Bath and North East Somerset Council.
  • Not considered to be at risk of flooding
  • Outside City of Bath World Heritage Site
  • Visual impact could be reduced by landscaping and planting
  • In public ownership
  • Requires a new access from the A4
  • Could not be served by a new rail station
